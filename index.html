吸烟污染：被忽视的生态威胁

Smoking Pollution: An Overlooked Ecological Threat

一个关于公共空间设计、行为规范与可持续解决方案的探究性研究项目。我们应用解放性设计思维，深入了解吸烟污染问题，并与利益相关者合作寻求切实可行的解决方案。

我们为了增加群众对于吸烟的了解所以我们决定宣传有关吸烟的危害（对社会，个人健康和自然界）。这个网站主要包含了我们小组做了关于这个项目的所有过程和一些有关吸烟的污染科普。

In order to enhance the public's understanding of smoking, we decided to promote the dangers of smoking (to society, personal health and the environment). This website mainly contains all the processes our team went through regarding this project, as well as some popular science information about smoking pollution.

项目概览 Project Brief

问题定义

在本地社区与校园周边，烟头是最常见却往往是最容易被忽视的垃圾之一。 人们并不了解烟头中含有塑料滤嘴和有毒残留物会长期污染土壤与水体和伤害微型生态系统。 而目前，现有环保宣传往往指责吸烟者“缺乏公德”，却未提供“可行且有效”的基础设施或理解吸烟者在公共空间中的真实处境，几乎全凭吸烟者自主的道德素养。 而本项目通过调查问卷和采访来观察与倾听不同使用者的声音，分析公共空间设计、垃圾管理与行为规范之间的关系，探索如何减少烟头污染、同时不影响个体的社区共同解决方案。

Problem Definition

In the local area, cigarette butts are litter that people usually ignore and forget, but they are the most collected waste. They are the most harmful pollutants at the same time. In a cigarette butt, there are materials like plastic, paper, and tobacco. Some of the substances are toxic. When a cigarette butt goes into water, about 2500 toxic chemicals are released. These poisons include arsenic, mercury, lead, tar, and pesticides. Current environmental protection campaigns often criticize smokers as being “lacking in public morality”, but do not provide a manageable and efficient way to understand smokers’ situations in public areas. As a result, this project aims to listen to diverse voices, analyze space management in public areas, and examine the relationship between garbage management and the code of conduct to explore how to reduce cigarette pollution and to create a common solution that every individual can benefit from by conducting surveys, interviews, field research, and other methods. (Referenced from Surfrider)

研究方法

本项目通过问卷调查和访谈，观察并倾听不同用户的声音，分析公共空间设计、垃圾管理与行为规范之间的关系，探索如何在减少烟头污染的同时不影响个人权利的社区共同解决方案。

Research Methodology

This project conducts surveys and interviews to observe and listen to different users' voices, analyze the relationship between public space design, waste management, and codes of conduct, and explore community solutions that reduce cigarette butt pollution without infringing on individual rights.

可持续性分析

个人层面 Personal Level

吸烟者的习惯、成瘾性、在公共空间的尴尬感；非吸烟者对环境和健康的担忧。

The habits of smokers, their addiction, the awkwardness they feel in public spaces; the concerns of non-smokers about the environment and health.

社区层面 Socity Level

校园/社区的清洁形象、公共健康文化、邻里关系、清洁成本。

The clean image of campus/community, public health culture, neighborhood relationships, and cleaning costs.

全球层面 Global Level

烟头是全球散落量第一的塑料垃圾；微塑料和有毒化学物质通过水循环进入全球生态系统。

Cigarette butts are the most littered plastic waste globally; microplastics and toxic chemicals enter global ecosystems through water cycles.

生态层面 Environmental Level

滤嘴（醋酸纤维素塑料）难以降解；尼古丁、重金属等毒素渗入土壤和水体，危害动植物。

Filter tips (cellulose acetate plastic) are difficult to biodegrade; nicotine, heavy metals, and other toxins seep into soil and water bodies, harming plants and animals.

研究过程 Research Process

Week 1-2

共情阶段 Emphasizing Period

在这两周，我们绘制了“利益相关者地图”和两张“未来轮”（Futures Wheel），并通过实地采访社会上（常熟）吸烟者、非吸烟者及UWC学生，收集多元声音，制定解决方案并设计产品原型。

During these two weeks, we created the "Stakeholder Map" and two "Futures Wheels", conducted on-site interviews with smokers, non-smokers in Changshu, and UWC students to gather diverse perspectives, formulate solutions, and design product prototypes.

Week 2-3

分析阶段 Analysis Period

基于问卷调查和共情阶段的研究，我们设计了三款产品，综合考虑，我们认为第二款产品得到的反馈更多元。我们注重改进了这款产品的功能和结构。

Based on the questionnaire survey and the empathy stage research, we designed three products. After comprehensive consideration, we believe that the second product received more diverse feedback. We focused on improving the functions and structure of this product.

Week 3-4

海报制作与展示阶段 Poster Making and Presentation Period

在3-4周，我们总结了我们的研究和设计成果，我们制作了展示海报和此网站并进行了项目展示，向校园社区和利益相关者分享我们的发现和解决方案。

During the period of 3 to 4 weeks, we summarized our research and design outcomes. We created presentation posters and this website, and conducted a project presentation to share our findings and solutions with the campus community and stakeholders.

主要发现 Main Findings

低吸烟率与社会规范

仅7%受访者有吸烟习惯，70%表示周围几乎无人或少数人吸烟，反映校园社区反吸烟规范环境强劲。非吸烟者对二手烟和烟头污染高度敏感。

Only 7% of respondents have a smoking habit, with 70% reporting few or no smokers around them, indicating a strong anti-smoking normative environment in the campus community. Non-smokers are highly sensitive to secondhand smoke and butt pollution.

产品偏好与反馈

37%首选产品1（安全戒烟辅助），30%选产品2（厌恶片剂），但反馈指出产品2可能因“难吃/不适”而采用率低；产品3被视为类似电子烟，效率存疑。

37% prefer Product 1 (safe quit aid), 30% choose Product 2 (aversion tablet), but feedback notes low adoption for Product 2 due to "unpleasant/uncomfortable" taste; Product 3 seen as e-cig-like, potentially inefficient.

危害认知与政策支持

65%认知环境影响（烟头毒素渗入水土），85%支持更严格政策（如禁公共场所、提高税、烟头设施），期望结合教育减少污名化。

65% recognize environmental impacts (butt toxins leaching into water/soil), 85% support stricter policies (e.g., public bans, tax hikes, butt collection facilities), expecting education to reduce stigma.

系统性问题 Systemic Issues

调研揭示烟头污染不仅是个人行为问题，更是系统设计与社会动态的产物：

  • 基础设施缺失：公共空间缺乏便捷烟头回收设施，导致乱扔率高（吸烟者中25%承认随手丢弃）。
  • 执法与教育不均衡：政策支持高但执行松散，需加强宣传以提升环境意识而非仅道德指责。
  • 污名化障碍：吸烟者感尴尬，影响负责任处置；解决方案应社区导向，避免加剧社会分歧。
  • 成瘾与替代挑战：低戒烟意愿（吸烟者中仅50%有计划），产品需结合心理/社会支持，而非单一工具。
  • Lack of infrastructure: The public spaces lack convenient facilities for cigarette butt recycling, resulting in a high rate of littering (25% of smokers admit to discarding them carelessly).
  • Inequality between law enforcement and education: Policy support is high but enforcement is loose, requiring stronger awareness campaigns rather than moral condemnation.
  • Stigmatization Disorder: Smokers feel embarrassed, affecting responsible disposal; solutions should be community-oriented to avoid exacerbating social divisions.
  • Addiction and Alternative Challenges: Low quit intention (only 50% of smokers have a plan), products need to combine psychological/social support rather than being standalone tools.

这些发现支持项目目标：通过教育+设施+产品，减少污染并提升社区福祉。（基于82份问卷+商场访谈）

These findings underscore the project's aim: reduce pollution and enhance community well-being via education + facilities + products. (Based on 82 surveys + mall interviews)

解决方案与建议

Solutions and Recommendations

原型解决方案 Prototype Solutions

三种产品原型整体草图（Product 1, 2, 3）
Overall Sketch of Three Product Prototypes

产品1 Product 1

一款分区装置，一半装水、一半装烟草。按下按钮，吸管尖端可戳破水与烟草间的隔膜，摇晃混合后含入口中约 2 分钟再吐出。使用者闻到烟味时会产生生理性厌恶，大脑会将这种恶心感与吸烟绑定，从而减少吸烟欲望。

A two‑chamber device: one half holds water, the other tobacco. Pressing a button allows a straw tip to pierce the membrane between them. After shaking and mixing, the liquid is held in the mouth for about 2 minutes then spat out. When users smell cigarette smoke, they feel physiological disgust, and the brain links this unpleasant sensation to smoking, helping reduce the urge to smoke.

产品2 Product 2

可食用的健康糖片，采用难吃但对身体有益的食材。原理与第一款相似：当人想吸烟时服用，身体产生的厌恶感会与吸烟欲望结合，促使大脑排斥吸烟行为。

The second product is an edible, healthy sugar tablet made with unpleasant‑tasting but beneficial ingredients. Similar to the first product, when taken before a craving, it triggers physical aversion. The brain then connects this unpleasant feeling with the desire to smoke, discouraging the behavior.

产品3 Product 3

外形与香烟一致的水雾装置，可将水或其他无害液体雾化喷出，不含有害气体。喷头朝下设计，避免水雾溅到他人，适合在社交与公共场所使用。

We created a cigarette‑shaped device that turns water or other harmless liquids into a fine mist, with no toxic gases. The nozzle points downward to prevent mist from splashing on others, making it suitable for social and public settings.

产品反馈与改进建议 Product Feedback and Improvement Recommendations

产品1反馈 Product 1 Feedback

无明显异议或负面评论。受访者认为其安全、实用，适合有戒烟意愿的用户。建议：加强临床安全验证，确保成分无害（符合伦理规范）。

No disagreements or major improvement comments. Respondents viewed it as safe and practical for motivated quitters. Suggestion: Enhance clinical safety validation to ensure harmless ingredients (ethical compliance).

产品2反馈 Product 2 Feedback

反馈声音多样：部分人认为“没人会买，因为不满足吸烟需求”；不愿“放嘴里不舒服”；建议“用好味替代厌恶口感”。受欢迎但采用疑虑高，反映公共卫生干预的阻力（如不适、污名）。

Diverse feedback: Some said "no one will buy it as it doesn't meet smokers' needs"; others unwilling due to "uncomfortable in mouth"; suggestion "use pleasant flavors instead." Popular in principle but adoption skepticism high, mirroring public health resistance (discomfort, stigma).

产品3反馈 Product 3 Feedback

被视为“类似于电子烟，人们更偏好电子烟，故低效”；“人们不会主动选择此类产品”。建议：增强独特过滤功能，避免与现有替代品重叠。

Seen as "similar to e-cigarettes, so people prefer e-cigs and it's inefficient"; "people won't actively choose it." Suggestion: Enhance unique filtration to differentiate from existing alternatives.

总体结论：对于产品1，鉴于伦理规则，我们需确保产品安全进入口腔。而烟草当中含有会对口腔物质有害的成分。对于产品3，人们可能会选择电子烟而不是我们的产品，市场方面不占优势，效果可能不好。所以综合考虑后，我们决定改进产品2，这个方案可行性高，创新性更能得到市场的认可，我们将就此方案将来进行深入研究与探索。

Overall Conclusion: For Product 1, after considering the ethical rules, we must ensure that the product safely enters the mouth. However, tobacco contains harmful things to a person’s mouth. For Product 3, people may choose e-cigarettes instead of our product, and it has no market advantage. The effect may also be poor. After careful consideration, we decided to improve Product 2. This plan is the most suitable one and has a greater innovation, which will be further studied and explored in the future based on this plan by our group.
